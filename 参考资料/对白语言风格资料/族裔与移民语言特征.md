# 族裔与移民语言特征 — 1920s 芝加哥

> 本文档为 NDC 项目（1925 年芝加哥背景）对白撰写参考资料。
> 核心原则：**芝加哥是一座移民城市**——1920年人口普查显示，芝加哥29%的居民是外国出生的，另有36%是移民的子女。每个族裔社区的英语都带有独特的语法、韵律和文化标记。

---

## 一、爱尔兰裔美国人

### 1.1 历史背景

到 1920s，爱尔兰裔已是芝加哥最有政治影响力的族裔群体之一。大饥荒移民（1840s-1850s）的第二三代已经完全美国化，但语言中仍保留鲜明的 Hiberno-English（爱尔兰英语）特征。

**聚居区域：** Bridgeport, Back of the Yards, Canaryville, Brighton Park
**职业分布：** 警察、消防员、政客、神职人员、工会领袖、码头工人、建筑工人

### 1.2 语法特征

**Hiberno-English 句式遗留：**

| 特征 | 标准英语 | 爱尔兰裔说法 | 语言学解释 |
|------|---------|------------|-----------|
| 进行时扩展 | "I know him" | "I'm knowing him" | 爱尔兰语用进行时表达状态 |
| after + 动名词 = 刚刚完成 | "I just saw him" | "I'm after seeing him" | 直译自爱尔兰语 Táim tar éis é a fheiceáil |
| 强调性倒装 | "It's a fine day" | "'Tis a fine day indeed" | 爱尔兰语的强调结构 |
| 反身代词强调 | "He did it" | "He did it himself" / "Himself did it" | himself/herself 作为第三人称指代 |
| 否定强调 | "I haven't" | "I have not" / "I did not, so I didn't" | 双重否定确认（与工人阶级双重否定不同） |
| 虚拟语气保留 | "if I were" | "if I was" / "were I to..." | 混用，视教育程度 |
| 所有格 on | "I have a cold" | "There's a cold on me" | 爱尔兰语介词所有格 |

### 1.3 常用表达与口头禅

**祝福与诅咒（爱尔兰裔最显著的语言特色）：**

```
# 祝福（blessing）：
"God bless you and keep you."
"May the road rise up to meet you."
"Saints preserve us."
"God willing." / "Please God."
"The Lord have mercy."
"God between us and all harm."

# 诅咒/感叹（oath）：
"Jaysus!" (Jesus 的爱尔兰发音)
"Jaysus, Mary, and Joseph!"
"Mother of God!"
"Holy Mother of —"
"Christ on a bike!" (较粗俗)
"Devil take him!"
"Bad cess to you!" (= 坏运降你)
"The devil and all his works!"

# 日常口头禅：
"Sure..." (句首填充词，几乎每句话都可能带)
"So it is." / "So it was." / "So I did." (确认性尾缀)
"Ah now..." (缓和性开头)
"Would you look at that, now."
"And isn't that the truth."
"Fair play to him." (= 给他公道)
"Mind yourself." (= 当心/保重)
"Thanks be to God." (感恩)
```

**爱尔兰裔特有的文学性修辞（gift of the gab）：**

```
"He could talk the hind legs off a donkey, that one."
"She has a tongue like a razor and a heart like a stone."
"He's as useful as a chocolate teapot."
"I wouldn't trust him as far as I could throw him."
"Sure, he'd steal the eye out of your head and come back for the eyebrow."
"That woman could start a fight in an empty room."
"He's got more stories than a library, and half of them true."
```

### 1.4 文化禁忌与敏感话题

```
# 绝对不能说的：
- 对爱尔兰人说 "Paddy" 或 "Mick" — 严重种族歧视（除非自己人开玩笑）
- 暗示爱尔兰人都是酒鬼 — 虽然饮酒文化确实存在，但这是侮辱
- 提及大饥荒时用"they should have grown something else" — 极度冒犯
- 把爱尔兰裔和英国裔混为一谈 — "I'm Irish, not British. There's a difference."

# 内部可以但外部不行的话题：
- 教会丑闻（内部批评可以，外人批评不行）
- IRA 和爱尔兰独立运动（1920s 正是爱尔兰内战期间，话题敏感）
- 家族内部矛盾（不外传）

# 爱尔兰裔对其他族裔的典型偏见表达（用于对话设计中展示时代偏见）：
"Those Italians — I don't trust a man I can't understand."
"The Poles keep to themselves. That's fine by me."
"At least we speak the language. We're Americans."
```

### 1.5 游戏对话示例

```
# 爱尔兰裔巡警：
"Now then, what's all this about? And don't be giving me any codswallop."
"I've been walking this beat for fifteen years, and I'm after seeing things that'd turn your hair white."
"Sure, I know every face on this block. That fella? Never seen him before, so I haven't."

# 爱尔兰裔政客：
"The people of this ward have my word — and an Irishman's word is his bond."
"Now, I'm not one to be pointing fingers, but certain individuals have been causing trouble in my precinct."
"Fair play to the man. He's done good work for the community."

# 爱尔兰裔母亲：
"Saints preserve us, what happened to your face? Come here and let me look at you."
"If your father finds out, there'll be hell to pay, so there will."
"Eat your dinner. There are children starving in — well, everywhere."
```

## 二、意大利裔美国人

### 2.1 历史背景

意大利移民主要来自南意大利（那不勒斯、西西里、卡拉布里亚），带来的是南意方言而非标准意大利语。到 1920s，第一代移民英语有限，第二代则在英语和意大利语间切换。

**聚居区域：** Near West Side (Little Italy / Taylor Street), Grand Avenue, Chinatown 附近
**职业分布：** 建筑工人、理发师、鞋匠、餐馆经营者、水果摊贩、部分进入有组织犯罪

### 2.2 第一代移民的英语特征

**系统性语法错误：**

| 特征 | 正确英语 | 意大利裔说法 | 原因 |
|------|---------|------------|------|
| 冠词缺失/错用 | "I went to the store" | "I go to store" | 意大利语冠词系统不同 |
| 动词时态简化 | "I worked yesterday" | "Yesterday I work" | 用副词替代时态标记 |
| 主语代词多余 | "The bread is good" | "The bread, she is good" | 意大利语有语法性别 |
| 双重否定 | "I don't know anything" | "I no know nothing" | 意大利语双重否定是标准用法 |
| is/are 混淆 | "They are" | "They is" | 简化变位 |
| 尾音添加 | "apartment" | "apartment-a" | 意大利语词汇以元音结尾 |
| word order | "a very big house" | "a house very big" | 意大利语形容词后置 |

### 2.3 常用意大利语嵌入词汇

这些意大利语词汇会自然嵌入英语对话中，特别是在情绪激动时：

```
# 称谓：
"paesano" — 同乡/老乡
"compare" / "goombah" (kumpa) — 教父/亲密朋友
"signore" / "signora" — 先生/女士
"bella" / "bello" — 美人/帅哥

# 感叹：
"Madonna!" — 圣母啊！（最常用的惊叹）
"Mamma mia!" — 我的妈呀！
"Dio mio!" — 我的天！
"Mannaggia!" — 该死！
"Per l'amor di Dio!" — 看在上帝份上！

# 日常词汇：
"mangiare" — 吃
"capisce?" — 明白吗？
"basta!" — 够了！
"andiamo" — 走吧
"grazie" — 谢谢
"prego" — 不客气
"salute" — 干杯/健康

# 骂人：
"stronzo" — 混蛋
"coglione" — 蠢蛋
"figlio di puttana" — 婊子养的（极粗俗）
"disgraziato" — 不要脸的人
```

### 2.4 意大利裔的文化价值观在语言中的体现

**家庭至上（La Famiglia）：**
```
"Family is everything. You don't turn your back on family."
"I swear on my mother's grave."
"My son, he's a good boy. He would never do such a thing."
"In my family, we no talk to police. We handle things ourselves."
"A man who doesn't take care of his family is not a man."
```

**荣誉与面子（Onore）：**
```
"You insult my family, you insult me."
"A man's word is his honor. I say I do, I do."
"Rispetto — respect. That's all I ask."
"You come into my house, you show respect."
"I no forget this. You understand? I no forget."
```

**沉默法则（Omertà）— 特别是涉及犯罪活动时：**
```
"I see nothing. I hear nothing. I know nothing."
"You want me to talk? Talk about what? I got nothing to say."
"In the old country, we have a saying — chi parla muore. He who talks, dies."
"I don't know no Torrio. I don't know no Capone. I'm just a baker."
```

### 2.5 第二代意大利裔（American-born）

第二代的英语流利但保留文化特征：

```
"My pop came over in '03. From Naples. Didn't speak a word of English."
"I'm American. Born and raised. But try telling that to the Irish cops."
"Sunday dinner at Mama's — you don't miss Sunday dinner. Ever."
"I speak Italian at home, English on the street. That's how it works."
"Don't call me a wop. I'll knock your teeth in."
```

### 2.6 文化禁忌

```
# 侮辱性称呼（绝不能用，除非角色是明确的种族主义者）：
"wop" — 最严重的反意大利裔蔑称
"dago" — 严重侮辱
"guinea" — 严重侮辱
"greaseball" — 侮辱（针对外貌）

# 地雷话题：
- 暗示所有意大利人都是黑帮 — "Not every Italian is a gangster. My father is a bricklayer."
- 侮辱意大利食物 — 这是真正的底线
- 批评某人的母亲 — 在意大利文化中这是开战
- 在餐桌上讨论不愉快话题 — 吃饭时间是神圣的
```

## 三、波兰裔美国人

### 3.1 历史背景

波兰移民在 1880s-1920s 大量涌入芝加哥，使芝加哥成为世界上最大的"波兰城市"之一（仅次于华沙和罗兹）。到 1920s，波兰社区已高度组织化，有自己的教堂、学校、报纸和社会机构。

**聚居区域：** Polish Triangle (Division/Milwaukee/Ashland), Wicker Park, Avondale, Bridgeport 部分
**职业分布：** 钢铁工人、屠宰场工人、建筑工人、工厂工人、少量小商人

### 3.2 波兰裔的英语特征

**系统性语法特点：**

| 特征 | 正确英语 | 波兰裔说法 | 原因 |
|------|---------|-----------|------|
| 冠词完全缺失 | "I work in a factory" | "I work in factory" | 波兰语无冠词 |
| th 发音替换 | "the thing" | "de ting" | 波兰语无 /θ/ 和 /ð/ 音 |
| w/v 混淆 | "we went" | "ve vent" | 波兰语 w 发 /v/ 音 |
| 介词混用 | "I'm at work" | "I am on work" / "I am by work" | 波兰语介词系统不同 |
| 时态简化 | "I have been working" | "I am working since five years" | 波兰语时态系统更简单 |
| 词序偏移 | "the big red house" | "the house big, red" | 波兰语形容词可后置 |
| have/has 困难 | "He has" | "He have" | 简化第三人称变位 |

### 3.3 常用波兰语嵌入词汇

```
# 称谓：
"Pan" / "Pani" — 先生/女士（极正式，日常也用）
"Panie" — 先生（呼格）

# 感叹：
"Jezus Maria!" — 耶稣玛利亚！（最常用惊叹）
"O Boże!" — 我的天！
"Matko Boska!" — 圣母啊！
"Cholera!" — 该死！（字面意思是霍乱）
"Kurde!" — 该死！（略粗俗）

# 日常词汇：
"tak" — 是
"nie" — 不
"dobrze" — 好/好的
"dziękuję" — 谢谢
"przepraszam" — 对不起/打扰了
"na zdrowie" — 干杯（为健康）
"kiełbasa" — 香肠（日常食物词汇常用波兰语）
"pierogi" — 饺子
```

### 3.4 波兰裔的文化特征在语言中的体现

**宗教虔诚（天主教）：**
```
"God is good. We work hard, God provides."
"Every Sunday, church. Rain or snow, church."
"Father Kowalski says we must pray for strength."
"I light candle for my mother. Every week, candle."
"In God we trust. In nobody else."
```

**工作伦理：**
```
"I work. That is what I do. I work."
"My hands — look — hard like wood. From work."
"In Poland, work. In America, work. But here, is better pay."
"Lazy man is no good. Polish man is never lazy."
"I take any job. Clean, dirty, I don't care. Work is work."
```

**社区与团结：**
```
"In Polish neighborhood, we help each other."
"You need something, you go to Polish club. They help."
"We stick together. Polish people, we stick together."
"The Irish cops, they don't understand us. They don't try."
"We have our own church, our own school, our own newspaper. We take care of ourselves."
```

### 3.5 文化禁忌

```
# 侮辱性称呼：
"Polack" — 最严重的反波兰裔蔑称（虽然 Polak 在波兰语中只是"波兰人"的意思）
"dumb Polack" — 极侮辱的刻板印象（暗示愚蠢）

# 地雷话题：
- 暗示波兰人愚蠢 — 1920s 已存在的刻板印象，极度令波兰裔愤怒
- 混淆波兰和俄罗斯 — "I am Polish, NOT Russian. Very different."
- 批评天主教会 — 对虔诚的波兰社区是极大冒犯
- 嘲笑波兰食物或传统 — 会被视为对整个文化的攻击
```

### 3.6 游戏对话示例

```
# 波兰裔工厂工人（第一代，英语有限）：
"I work in factory. Stockyard. Is hard, but I no complain."
"The boss, he say come early. So I come. What else I do?"
"My boy, he go to school. Good school. American school. He will be better than me."
"Please, I no want trouble. I just want work, feed my family."

# 波兰裔小店主（第二代，英语流利）：
"My father opened this shop in 1905. I took it over when he got sick."
"We sell Polish sausage, Polish bread, Polish everything. Best on Division Street."
"I speak English fine, but at home it's Polish. Mama won't have it any other way."
```

## 四、犹太裔美国人

### 4.1 历史背景

东欧犹太移民（来自俄罗斯帝国、波兰、罗马尼亚）在 1880s-1920s 大量来到芝加哥。到 1920s，第一代仍说 Yiddish，第二代是 Yiddish-English 双语者。

**聚居区域：** Maxwell Street (Near West Side) → 1920s 开始迁往 Lawndale (North Lawndale)
**职业分布：** 裁缝、小商人（especially Maxwell Street 集市）、废品收购、皮草业、小型制造业、部分进入法律和医学

### 4.2 Yiddish-English 的语言特征

**最显著的句式特征——倒装强调：**
```
"A doctor he wants to be." (= He wants to be a doctor)
"Money I don't have." (= I don't have money)
"Trouble, who needs it?" (= Who needs trouble?)
"Such a mess you never saw." (= You never saw such a mess)
"Beautiful she isn't, but smart? Like a whip."
"This he calls a deal?" (= He calls this a deal?)
```

**反问句式（rhetorical questions）——犹太裔英语的标志：**
```
"So what am I, chopped liver?"
"You call this a living?"
"This is what I get for my trouble?"
"What do I know from [something]?" (= What do I know about [something])
"Who asked you?"
"Is this any way to treat a customer?"
"What, I should be happy about this?"
"Am I my brother's keeper?" (圣经引用融入日常)
"You want I should cry?"
```

**"So" 作为万能连接词：**
```
"So, what else is new?"
"So he comes to me, he says..."
"So I told him, I said..."
"So what?"
"So?" (= 然后呢？/ 那又怎样？)
"So nu?" (= So? / Well? — nu 是 Yiddish 语气词)
```

### 4.3 高频 Yiddish 嵌入词汇

这些词汇中很多后来进入了美国英语主流：

| Yiddish 词 | 含义 | 例句 |
|-----------|------|------|
| **mensch** | 正人君子/品行端正的人 | "Be a mensch. Do the right thing." |
| **schmuck** | 蠢蛋/混蛋（粗俗） | "Don't be a schmuck." |
| **schlemiel** | 笨手笨脚的倒霉鬼 | "That schlemiel spilled soup on the customer." |
| **schlemazel** | 运气差的人 | "The schlemiel spills the soup, the schlemazel gets it in his lap." |
| **chutzpah** | 厚脸皮/胆大妄为 | "The chutzpah of that man! Asking for a raise after what he did!" |
| **kvetch** | 抱怨/抱怨的人 | "Stop kvetching and do something about it." |
| **kibitz** | 旁观指点/闲聊 | "He's just kibitzing. Ignore him." |
| **nosh** | 吃零食 | "Come, nosh a little. You're too thin." |
| **tchotchke** | 小玩意儿/装饰品 | "The shelf is full of tchotchkes." |
| **meshuggeneh** | 疯的/疯子 | "The whole world is going meshuggeneh." |
| **shtick** | 把戏/招牌动作 | "That's his shtick — the sad face, the sob story." |
| **gonif** | 小偷/骗子 | "That gonif charged me double!" |
| **maven** | 专家 | "Ask Saul — he's a maven on real estate." |
| **oy vey** | 哎呀（感叹） | "Oy vey, what a day." |
| **bubbeleh** | 亲爱的（对孩子/亲近的人） | "Come here, bubbeleh. Let me look at you." |
| **bubbe** | 奶奶/外婆 | "My bubbe always said..." |
| **tsouris** | 麻烦/烦恼 | "Nothing but tsouris from that boy." |
| **shmooze** | 闲聊/社交性谈话 | "He can shmooze with anybody." |
| **zaftig** | 丰满的（形容女性体型，带赞美意） | "She's a little zaftig, but a beauty." |
| **klutz** | 笨拙的人 | "Watch where you're going, you klutz." |

### 4.4 犹太裔文化价值观的语言表达

**学习/教育至上：**
```
"My boy is going to be a doctor. Or a lawyer. Preferably both."
"An education is the one thing they can never take from you."
"Books — books are the real treasure."
"Study, study, study. That's what I tell my children."
```

**幽默与自嘲：**
```
"I'm so broke, I can't even pay attention."
"My luck? If I sold umbrellas, it would stop raining."
"I asked God for a sign. He sent me a bill."
"If I had a dollar for every time life kicked me, I'd be a Rockefeller."
"Laugh or cry — I prefer to laugh. Crying gives me a headache."
```

**商业文化的语言痕迹：**
```
"Let's talk business. Talk is cheap, but my time isn't."
"I'll make you a deal you can't refuse. Well, you can refuse, but why would you?"
"The customer is always right. Even when he's wrong, he's right."
"In business, trust but verify. Actually, just verify."
"A handshake deal — what's better than a handshake?"
```

### 4.5 文化禁忌

```
# 侮辱性称呼：
"kike" — 最严重的反犹蔑称（来源有争议，可能源于 kikel = 圆圈，犹太移民签名时画圆圈代替十字）
"Hebe" / "Hymie" — 侮辱性称呼
"Shylock" — 将犹太人等同于高利贷者，严重冒犯

# 地雷话题（1920s语境）：
- 关于金钱的刻板印象 — "Not every Jew is a banker. My father is a tailor."
- 忠诚度质疑 — 暗示犹太人不是"真正的美国人"
- 饮食嘲笑 — 嘲笑 kosher 规矩
- 大屠杀之前的反犹主义 — 1920s 的反犹情绪已经很严重（KKK 反犹、亨利·福特反犹宣传）
```

## 五、非裔美国人

### 5.1 历史背景

1920s 正是"大迁徙"（Great Migration）的高峰期。数十万非裔美国人从南方（密西西比、阿拉巴马、路易斯安那、阿肯色）迁往芝加哥，寻求工厂工作和逃离种族隔离。芝加哥的 South Side（特别是 Bronzeville / Black Belt）成为全美最大的黑人社区之一。

**聚居区域：** South Side (Bronzeville / Black Belt) — State Street 从 22nd 到 63rd
**职业分布：** 钢铁工人、屠宰场工人、铁路搬运工（Pullman porter）、家政服务、音乐家（爵士乐）、部分小商人和专业人士（黑人中产阶级在 Bronzeville 兴起）

### 5.2 语言特征

1920s 非裔美国人英语（African American English / AAE）已经形成了独特的语言系统。重要提醒：**AAE 不是"错误的英语"，而是有自己完整语法规则的语言变体。**

**核心语法特征：**

| 特征 | 标准英语 | AAE 说法 | 说明 |
|------|---------|---------|------|
| copula 省略 | "He is working" | "He working" | 系动词 is/are 在特定环境可省略 |
| habitual be | "He is always working" | "He be working" | 表示习惯性动作（非错误） |
| done 完成体 | "He has already finished" | "He done finished" | 表示已完成 |
| been 远过去 | "He has been married for a long time" | "He been married" | 强调持续很久 |
| 否定一致 | "He doesn't know anything" | "He don't know nothing" | 双重/多重否定 |
| 第三人称 -s 省略 | "He goes" | "He go" | |
| it/they 存在句 | "There is..." | "It's a..." / "They got..." | |

### 5.3 1920s 芝加哥非裔美国人的独特语言背景

从南方来的移民带来了南方方言特征，在芝加哥的城市环境中与北方英语混合：

**南方遗留特征：**
```
"I been knowing him since way back." (= I've known him a long time)
"I'm fixing to leave." (= I'm about to leave) — 南方方言
"She stay cooking." (= She's always cooking) — habitual
"He come telling me..." (= He came and told me)
"I ain't studyin' you." (= I'm not paying attention to you / I don't care)
"Don't mess with me none." (= Don't mess with me)
```

**城市化后的新表达：**
```
"The South Side is jumping tonight." (= 热闹/活跃)
"I'm making good money at the plant." (= 工厂)
"Up here, at least they pay you fair. Mostly."
"Back home wasn't nothing for us. That's why we came."
"Chicago — the Promised Land, they called it. Well, promises can be broken."
```

### 5.4 爵士乐文化与非裔美国人语言

Bronzeville 是 1920s 爵士乐的核心地带之一，音乐文化深刻影响了语言：

```
# 音乐圈用语：
"That cat can blow." (= 那个乐手演奏得好)
"The band is really cooking tonight."
"He's got soul. You can't teach that."
"She's singing from somewhere deep. That ain't just music — that's truth."
"After the set, we'll jam."

# 从音乐延伸到日常的词汇：
"That's hip." / "You hip to that?" (= 你知道/了解吗)
"Don't be a square."
"Solid." (= 好的/可靠的)
"Dig it." (= 理解/欣赏它) — 1920s 已在使用
```

### 5.5 种族关系中的语言动态

**与白人对话时的"面具语言"（code-switching）：**

1920s 的非裔美国人在与白人交流时常使用一种更正式、更"标准"的英语，这是一种自我保护策略（W.E.B. Du Bois 的"双重意识"概念）：

```
# 对白人雇主/白人权威：
"Yes, sir. Right away, sir."
"I'm just doing my job, sir. Don't want any trouble."
"I appreciate the opportunity, Mr. Henderson."

# 同社区内（完全不同的语言自由度）：
"Man, you hear what happened on State Street? They shot up the barbershop."
"I ain't going back down South for nothing. Nothing."
"The Man don't care about us. Never did, never will."
"We got to look out for our own. Ain't nobody else gonna do it."
```

**非裔美国人面对种族偏见时的语言策略：**

```
# 沉默与服从（自我保护）：
"I don't want any trouble, sir." (重复——强调和平意图)
[沉默] (有时沉默本身就是最安全的回应)

# 尊严性的间接抗议：
"I'm a man, same as you."
"I fought for this country. France, 1918. I'd like to be treated accordingly."
"My money's as green as anybody else's."

# 幽默作为武器：
"You don't like my kind? Well, I'm not too fond of yours either, but you don't see me making a fuss."
"I'd sit at the front of the bus, but I hear the back's got a better view."
```

### 5.6 文化禁忌与语言注意事项

```
# 游戏设计中的处理原则：
- N-word：1920s 被白人广泛使用的种族蔑称。在游戏中可以通过暗示存在但不直接展示，
  或仅在最必要的情况下出现以反映时代真实性，需极度谨慎处理。
- "boy"：白人对成年非裔男性的称呼，极具侮辱性——剥夺成年人身份
- "colored"：1920s 的"official"用语（NAACP 中的 C），但带有居高临下
- "Negro"：当时被认为是正式/尊重的称呼（W.E.B. Du Bois 使用）
- "Auntie" / "Uncle"：白人对年长非裔美国人的称呼，否认其姓名的尊严

# 非裔社区内部的语言细微差别：
- 南方出生 vs 芝加哥出生的非裔美国人之间有微妙的地位差异
- "old settler"（早期迁来的中产阶级非裔）vs "newcomer"（大迁徙新来者）
- 肤色深浅在社区内部的敏感性（colorism 问题）
```

### 5.7 Pullman Porter（铁路搬运工）的特殊语言

Pullman Porter 是 1920s 非裔美国人中一个特殊且重要的职业群体。他们的语言融合了服务业的礼貌和黑人社区的智慧：

```
"Right this way, sir. I'll take your bags."
"Is there anything else I can get for you, ma'am?"
"I've been running this line for twelve years. Chicago to New York and back."
"You hear a lot on the train. People forget we're in the room."
"They call us all 'George' — after George Pullman. As if we don't have names."
"The tips are good if you smile right. So I smile."
```

## 六、德裔美国人（简要补充）

### 6.1 背景

德裔美国人曾是芝加哥最大的族裔群体之一，但一战期间的反德情绪（1917-1918）导致许多德裔隐藏身份。到 1920s，公开使用德语变得敏感。

**语言特征（保留但被压抑）：**
```
# 语法干扰（有时会暴露德裔背景）：
"I am here already five years." (= I've been here five years) — 德语语序
"The meeting is at Tuesday." (= on Tuesday) — 介词干扰
"I have it already done." (= I've already done it) — 德语完成时词序

# 文化词汇偶尔闪现：
"Gemütlichkeit" — 舒适/温馨
"Kindergarten" — 已完全英语化
"Gesundheit" — 有人打喷嚏时说
"Wanderlust" — 旅行欲
"Kaput" — 坏了/完了

# 战后自我审查：
"My family is from — well, we've been here a long time."
"Schmidt? No, it's Smith. Always been Smith." (改了姓)
"I don't — I'd rather not discuss the old country."
```

## 七、族裔交互场景的对话设计参考

### 7.1 不同族裔角色相遇时的典型语言摩擦

```
# 爱尔兰裔警察 vs 意大利裔商人：
Cop: "You got a license for this cart, Tony?"
Tony: "My name is not Tony. Is Antonio. And yes, I have license."
Cop: "Sure, sure. Antonio. Let me see it, then."
Tony: "Why you always come to me? The Irish man on corner, he have no license. You no bother him."
Cop: "That's different."
Tony: "Different how?"

# 犹太裔商人 vs 波兰裔工人：
Merchant: "Two dollars for the coat. It's practically new."
Worker: "Two dollars? Is too much. I give you one."
Merchant: "One dollar? For this coat? What am I, a charity? One-fifty, and I'm losing money."
Worker: "One dollar and twenty cents. Final."
Merchant: "You're killing me. You're absolutely killing me. Fine. One-twenty. Take it."

# 非裔音乐家 vs 白人俱乐部经理：
Manager: "You boys play good. Real good."
Musician: "We're men, not boys. And we play great."
Manager: "Right, right. No offense. You got the gig. Tuesdays and Thursdays. Back entrance."
Musician: "Back entrance."
Manager: "That's how it is. Take it or leave it."
Musician: "... We'll take it. For now."
```

### 7.2 族裔背景作为推理线索

在侦探游戏中，角色的族裔语言特征可以作为线索：

```
# 伪装身份的破绽：
"He says he's Irish, but he said 'th' perfectly. No Irishman from Bridgeport says 'th' that clean."
"She claims to be American-born, but she said 'I have hunger' instead of 'I'm hungry.' That's Italian structure."
"He says he doesn't speak Yiddish, but he said 'nu' twice. That's not something you pick up by accident."

# 社区知识作为证据：
"Nobody calls it 'the Polish Triangle' except outsiders. We call it 'the neighborhood.'"
"He said he lives on Maxwell Street, but he didn't know about the Sunday market. Everybody on Maxwell knows the market."
```
