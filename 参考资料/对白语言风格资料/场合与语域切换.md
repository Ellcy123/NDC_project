# 场合与语域切换 — 1920s 美国/芝加哥

> 本文档为 NDC 项目（1925 年芝加哥背景）对白撰写参考资料。
> 核心原则：**同一个人在不同场合说不同的话**——语域（register）切换是 1920s 社会的基本生存技能。在禁酒令、阶级分化、种族隔离的多重压力下，人们需要在多个"语言身份"之间灵活切换。

---

## 一、正式场合

### 1.1 法庭

1920s 的法庭是最正式的语言环境之一。所有参与者都被期望使用高度规范化的语言。

**法官的语言：**
```
"This court is now in session. All rise."
"The defendant will rise and face the jury."
"Counselor, you will confine your remarks to the matter at hand."
"I will remind the gallery that outbursts will not be tolerated."
"The court finds the defendant guilty / not guilty."
"Sentence will be pronounced at a date to be determined."
"This court stands adjourned."
```

**律师的正式陈述语言：**
```
"May it please the court..."
"Ladies and gentlemen of the jury, the evidence will show..."
"I submit to this court that the prosecution has failed to establish..."
"With the court's permission, I should like to call my next witness."
"I respectfully object to counsel's characterization of the evidence."
"The defense rests, Your Honor."
```

**证人的正式语言（与日常形成强烈反差）：**
```
# 被律师引导的正式回答：
"Yes, sir, I was present at the location on the evening in question."
"To the best of my recollection, the individual entered at approximately nine o'clock."
"I cannot say with certainty, sir."

# 同一个证人在日常中的说法：
"Yeah, I was there that night."
"He showed up around nine, I guess."
"Beats me."
```

**法庭语言的核心规则：**
- 永远用 "sir" / "ma'am" / "Your Honor"
- 不使用俚语（律师可能故意使用以显得亲民，但这是策略）
- 不使用缩略形式："I did not" 而非 "I didn't"
- 间接回答会被法官制止："A simple yes or no, please."
- 证人可以说 "I don't recall" 但不能说 "I forgot"（后者暗示记忆力差）

### 1.2 商务会议 / 银行

**银行经理的语言：**
```
"Good morning, Mr. Whitmore. How may the bank be of service today?"
"I'm afraid the terms of the loan are non-negotiable."
"The bank requires collateral of equal or greater value."
"Your account balance as of the first was... let me see... satisfactory."
"We pride ourselves on the confidentiality of our clients' affairs."
"The board of directors has reviewed your application, and I'm pleased to say..."
"I should caution you, Mr. Henderson, that the current market conditions..."
```

**商务谈判用语：**
```
"Shall we get down to brass tacks?" (= 谈正事)
"I believe we can reach a mutually beneficial arrangement."
"My associates and I have discussed the matter, and our position is firm."
"The numbers speak for themselves."
"I'll need that in writing."
"We have a gentleman's agreement, do we not?"
"I trust your word, but business is business."
"Let me be perfectly clear about our terms."
```

### 1.3 社交晚宴 / 上流社会聚会

这是"表演式正式"——看似轻松但实际上每句话都经过权衡。

**到达与问候：**
```
"Good evening. What a delightful affair."
"How kind of you to include us."
"You look absolutely radiant this evening, Mrs. Palmer."
"Charmed, I'm sure." (标准初次见面用语)
"I don't believe we've been introduced. I'm —"
"The pleasure is entirely mine."
```

**餐桌对话（安全话题）：**
```
"Have you been to the opera this season? The new Puccini is extraordinary."
"I understand you're a collector. What period interests you?"
"The weather has been simply dreadful, hasn't it?"
"And how are the children? Growing like weeds, I imagine."
"Do tell me about your trip to the Continent."
```

**餐桌对话禁忌（不能讨论的话题）：**
```
# 绝不讨论：
- 金钱具体数字
- 疾病细节
- 政治（除非都是同一阵营）
- 宗教争议
- 任何"不愉快"的话题（犯罪、贫困、种族）
- 禁酒令（即使桌上正在喝酒——这是公开的秘密但不说出来）

# 如何优雅地回避：
"I'm afraid that's rather outside my area of expertise."
"Perhaps this isn't the time or place for such a discussion."
"Shall we change the subject? I find all this talk rather dreary."
"I'm sure there are those far more qualified than I to comment on such matters."
```

**告辞：**
```
"I'm afraid we must be going. It's been a wonderful evening."
"Thank you so much for a lovely time. You must come to us next."
"I've had a marvelous time, truly. You're too kind."
"I shall look forward to seeing you at the club."
```

## 二、半正式场合

### 2.1 办公室

**上级对下级：**
```
"Close the door. We need to talk."
"I expect that report on my desk by Monday morning."
"That's not a suggestion — it's an instruction."
"You've done good work, Henderson. Keep it up."
"If this happens again, there will be consequences."
"I'm going to be straight with you..."
"Between us — this doesn't leave this room."
```

**下级对上级：**
```
"You wanted to see me, sir?"
"I'll have it done by end of day."
"If I might make a suggestion, sir..."
"With respect, sir, I think there might be another way to look at this."
"I appreciate the opportunity, sir." (无论是否真心)
"Is there anything else you need, sir?"
```

**同事之间：**
```
"Did you hear about Johnson? He got canned."
"The boss is in a mood today. Watch yourself."
"You going to O'Malley's after work?"
"Keep your head down — layoffs are coming."
"I'm not getting paid enough for this."
"Cover for me at three, will you? I've got a dentist appointment."
"Who's bringing the flask? The afternoon meeting's going to be murder."
```

### 2.2 警局

**正式记录语言：**
```
"At approximately 2300 hours, officers responded to a disturbance at..."
"The suspect was apprehended at the corner of State and Madison."
"A search of the premises revealed..."
"The witness stated that she observed..."
"The body was discovered by the building custodian at 0615 hours."
```

**审讯室（半正式，带压力）：**
```
# Good cop:
"Look, I'm trying to help you here. But you gotta meet me halfway."
"Talk to me. Tell me what happened. In your own words."
"You seem like a decent fella. Let's figure this out together."
"Nobody wants to see you go down for this. But you gotta tell the truth."

# Bad cop:
"You're lying, and we both know it."
"I've got three witnesses who put you at the scene."
"You want to play games? Fine. We can play games all night."
"The DA wants to throw the book at you. I'm the only thing standing between you and Joliet."
```

**警察之间（完全非正式）：**
```
"This stiff ain't gonna solve itself."
"Another gangland hit. File it and forget it."
"The captain wants results. I want a raise. Nobody's getting what they want."
"I got a tip from a snitch on the South Side."
"Who's buying lunch? I'm broke till Friday."
"Twenty years on the force and every dead body still gets to me."
```

### 2.3 报社编辑部

```
# 编辑对记者（指令性）：
"I need six hundred words by four. Can you do it or not?"
"Your lead is buried. Rewrite."
"The publisher killed the story. Don't ask why."
"Fact-check everything. We got burned last month."

# 记者之间（竞争中的同志情谊）：
"I'm working a story that'll blow this town wide open."
"You chasing the Henderson murder too? Stay off my beat."
"Buy you a drink and you tell me what you know?"
"This job is going to kill me, but what else am I going to do?"
"The truth doesn't sell papers. Scandal sells papers."
```

### 2.4 医院 / 诊所

```
# 医生对病人（权威但人性化）：
"You're going to be fine, but you need rest."
"How long have you been experiencing these symptoms?"
"I'm going to be frank with you, Mr. Henderson."
"I'd like to keep you overnight for observation."
"This is going to require surgery. I won't sugarcoat it."

# 医生对护士（简短命令）：
"Nurse, I need gauze. Now."
"What's his temperature?"
"Prep for surgery. We don't have time."
"Keep him comfortable. That's all we can do."

# 病人（语域因身份而异）：
"Doc, give it to me straight. How bad is it?"  (工人阶级)
"I'd appreciate your honest assessment, Doctor."  (中产/上流)
"Am I gonna make it?"  (通用——极端情况下阶级差异消失)
```

## 三、非正式场合

### 3.1 地下酒吧 (Speakeasy)

speakeasy 是 1920s 社交的核心场所，也是阶级混合的独特空间——富人、工人、黑帮、艺术家、记者在同一个空间饮酒。语言因此特别丰富。

**入场暗语系统：**
```
# 门口互动（严肃）：
Doorman: "Who sent you?"
Patron: "Joe sent me." / "I'm a friend of Charlie's."
Doorman: (打量) "Password?"
Patron: "Chrysanthemum." / "Swordfish." / "New York."
Doorman: (开门) "Keep it down in there."

# 如果来了陌生人：
Doorman: "Sorry, this is a private club."
Stranger: "I was told—"
Doorman: "You was told wrong. Beat it."
```

**吧台对话：**
```
"What'll it be?"
"Whiskey. The real stuff, not that bathtub garbage."
"We got Canadian rye. Two bits a glass."
"Put it on my tab." / "I'm paying cash."
"Hit me again." (= 再来一杯)
"Make it a double."
"What's in this? ... Actually, don't tell me."
"To better days." (举杯祝词)
"Bottoms up."
"Here's mud in your eye." (1920s 祝酒词)
"Down the hatch."
```

**speakeasy 中的社交互动：**
```
# 搭讪：
"Buy you a drink?"
"I couldn't help but notice you from across the room."
"You here alone? ... Me too. What a coincidence."
"You come here often? Sorry, that's a terrible line. Let me try again."

# 被搭讪（拒绝）：
"I'm waiting for someone."
"My husband's in the men's room." (可能是真的也可能不是)
"Not tonight, Romeo."
"Do I know you? ... No? Let's keep it that way."

# 朋友之间：
"You look like you need a drink. Rough day?"
"Don't look now, but your wife's ex just walked in."
"I heard the most incredible thing today..."
"Keep your voice down — that's O'Banion's man at the bar."
```

### 3.2 家中 / 家庭场景

家庭场景中的语言最接近角色的"真实自我"——没有表演需要。

**丈夫下班回家：**
```
"I'm home. What's for dinner?"
"You wouldn't believe the day I had."
"Where are the kids?"
"Don't nag me, Helen. Not tonight."
"I brought home a little something." (可能是偷偷带的酒)
```

**妻子在家：**
```
"Dinner's almost ready. Go wash up."
"The children have been impossible today."
"We need to talk about the bills."
"Your mother called. Three times."
"There's a letter for you. From the bank."
```

**亲子对话：**
```
# 父亲（权威，有距离感）：
"Mind your mother."
"Don't make me tell you twice."
"When I was your age..."
"Someday you'll understand."
"I'm doing this for your own good."

# 母亲（温暖但严厉）：
"Come here, let me look at you. Are you hurt?"
"What did I tell you about fighting?"
"Eat your vegetables. Children are starving in Europe."
"Wait till your father gets home."
"I love you, but you're driving me crazy."
```

**兄弟姐妹之间（最不设防的语言）：**
```
"You're such a tattletale."
"I'm telling Ma."
"Move over, that's my chair."
"If you tell anyone, I'll murder you." (夸张，非真正威胁)
"Are you okay? ... Don't lie to me. I can tell."
"I covered for you last time. You owe me."
```

### 3.3 朋友间的私密对话

**男性朋友之间（酒吧、工会活动、打牌时）：**
```
"I'm in trouble, Mac. Real trouble."
"How much? ... That much? Christ."
"You can't tell anybody about this. Anybody."
"Remember that thing I told you about? It got worse."
"She wants a divorce. ... Yeah. ... I don't know what to do."
"I need a favor. A big one. No questions asked."
"You're my oldest friend. Don't make me beg."
```

**女性朋友之间（家访、教会后、购物时）：**
```
"Can I tell you something? Promise you won't tell a soul."
"He's been coming home late every night. I'm worried."
"I think I might be... you know." (暗示怀孕)
"She's seeing someone. Don't ask me who — I promised."
"I just need to talk to someone. I can't keep this inside anymore."
"What would you do? If you were me?"
```

## 四、对上 / 对下 / 对等的语言权力差异

### 4.1 语言权力的标记

1920s 的权力关系通过以下语言标记体现：

| 权力标记 | 上位者 | 下位者 |
|---------|-------|-------|
| **称谓** | 用名（John）或绰号 | 用 Mr./Mrs./Sir/Ma'am |
| **命令形式** | 直接命令或暗示 | 请求或建议 |
| **打断** | 可以随时打断 | 等待对方说完 |
| **话题控制** | 决定谈什么 | 跟随话题 |
| **沉默** | 沉默是威权展示 | 沉默是服从或恐惧 |
| **提问** | 要求回答 | 回答时小心措辞 |
| **幽默** | 可以开下位者的玩笑 | 只能自嘲 |

### 4.2 具体场景的权力语言

**老板 vs 员工：**
```
Boss: "Johnson, my office. Now."
Employee: "Yes, sir. Right away, sir."

Boss: "You've been late three times this week."
Employee: "I'm terribly sorry, sir. It won't happen again."

Boss: "See that it doesn't. Dismissed."
Employee: "Thank you, sir." (为什么说谢谢？因为没被开除)
```

**黑帮老大 vs 手下：**
```
Boss: "Sit down."
Thug: (坐下，不说话，等老大先说)

Boss: "I hear you've been talking."
Thug: "Boss, I swear on my mother—"
Boss: "Don't. Don't swear on your mother. Just listen."
Thug: (沉默)
Boss: "This is your one chance. Don't waste it."
Thug: "Whatever you say, boss. Whatever you say."
```

**医生 vs 病人（1920s 的医疗父权主义）：**
```
Doctor: "You need surgery. I've made the arrangements."
Patient: "But Doctor, isn't there another—"
Doctor: "I've examined you thoroughly. Surgery is the only option."
Patient: "When?"
Doctor: "Tomorrow morning. Don't eat after midnight."
Patient: "... Yes, Doctor."
# (病人几乎没有质疑空间——这在 1920s 是正常的)
```

**富人 vs 仆人（最极端的权力差异）：**
```
Mistress: "Mary, the silver needs polishing. Again."
Maid: "Yes, ma'am. I'll see to it at once."
Mistress: "And the flowers in the dining room are wilting. Replace them."
Maid: "Yes, ma'am."
Mistress: "That will be all."
Maid: "Ma'am." (退出)

# 仆人之间（厨房里）：
"The old bat wants the silver polished AGAIN. What does she do, count the fingerprints?"
"If she rings that bell one more time, I'm going to stuff it down her throat."
"Smile, nod, say yes ma'am. That's the job."
```

### 4.3 平等关系中的微妙博弈

即使在"平等"关系中，也存在语言博弈：

**两个律师（势均力敌）：**
```
A: "I think you'll find the precedent is against you, Counselor."
B: "Precedent can be overturned. That's what makes the law interesting."
A: "Your confidence is admirable, if somewhat misplaced."
B: "We'll see what the jury thinks."
A: "Indeed we shall."
```

**两个黑帮老大的会面（紧张的礼貌）：**
```
A: "Thank you for coming. I appreciate the gesture."
B: "The gesture is for the sake of business. Let's not pretend we're friends."
A: "Of course. Business, then."
B: "Your people have been operating on my side of the river."
A: "A misunderstanding. I'll correct it."
B: "See that you do. I'd hate for things to get... complicated."
A: "Nobody wants complications."
B: "Then we understand each other."
```

## 五、谎言与回避的语言策略

### 5.1 直接撒谎的语言模式

1920s 的谎言有特定的语言标记（对侦探游戏设计极其重要）：

**过度细节（guilty of over-explaining）：**
```
# 可疑的详细：
"I was at home, reading a book — Hemingway, actually, 'In Our Time' — my wife can confirm,
she was knitting in the next room, and at around nine-thirty I went to bed. I remember
because the clock on the mantelpiece chimed the half-hour."

# 对比正常的简短：
"I was home. My wife was there. I went to bed around nine-thirty."
```

**时间的模糊与精确不一致：**
```
# 对不重要的细节记得太清楚：
"I left the office at exactly five-seventeen."

# 对关键时间点突然模糊：
"After that... I'm not sure. The evening's a bit of a blur."
```

**被动句逃避主体：**
```
"The gun was fired." (谁开的枪？)
"Things got out of hand." (谁让事情失控的？)
"It happened." (什么怎么发生的？)
"Mistakes were made." (谁犯的错？)
"He was found that way." (谁发现的？在什么情况下？)
```

### 5.2 回避策略

**答非所问（deflection）：**
```
Detective: "Where were you Tuesday night?"
Suspect: "Tuesday? Let me think... I've been so busy lately. You know how it is."
Detective: "I need a specific answer."
Suspect: "Well, I'm sure I was somewhere perfectly ordinary. Is this about the Henderson thing?"
# (用问题回答问题，转移话题)
```

**反客为主（reversal）：**
```
"Why are you asking ME? Shouldn't you be talking to his business partner?"
"I find it curious that I'm being questioned when the real criminal is still out there."
"Before I answer that, perhaps you can tell me why you think I'm involved."
```

**装糊涂（playing dumb）：**
```
"I'm sorry, I'm not sure I understand the question."
"Henderson? The name doesn't ring a bell."
"I really don't follow. Could you be more specific?"
"I'm just a simple businessman. I don't know anything about these things."
```

**受害者姿态（victim posture）：**
```
"I can't believe you'd even suggest such a thing."
"After everything I've done for this community?"
"This is harassment, pure and simple."
"I'm the victim here, not the criminal."
"My reputation — my family's reputation — is at stake."
```

### 5.3 1920s 特有的委婉语

**死亡的委婉说法：**
```
"He passed." / "He passed on." / "He's gone."
"She's no longer with us."
"He met his maker."
"He's in a better place." (宗教语境)
"He was taken from us."
"He didn't make it."

# 更直接但仍有距离的说法：
"He was killed." (被动，避免指定凶手)
"Somebody did him in." (俚语，有距离感)

# 黑帮圈的委婉语：
"He had an accident." (= 被杀了)
"He went away." (= 被杀了或被消失了)
"He's sleeping with the fishes." (这个据说来自西西里黑手党传统)
"He took a long vacation." (= 坐牢或被杀)
```

**犯罪活动的委婉说法：**
```
"Business interests" (= 犯罪活动)
"Associates" (= 同伙)
"A misunderstanding" (= 暴力冲突)
"He was persuaded to cooperate." (= 被胁迫)
"We came to an arrangement." (= 行贿/交易)
"The matter has been resolved." (= 某人被杀/被收买/被威胁)
"He's in the import business." (= 走私)
"A private social club" (= speakeasy)
"Medicinal spirits" (= 非法酒水)
```

**身体/健康的委婉说法：**
```
"She's in a delicate condition." (= 怀孕)
"He's indisposed." (= 生病/喝醉/不想见人)
"He's not himself today." (= 喝醉了/情绪不好)
"She took to her bed." (= 病倒/崩溃)
"He's been under the weather." (= 生病)
"She's having a spell." (= 头晕/恐慌发作/歇斯底里)
"The sanitarium" (= 精神病院/酗酒戒断所)
"He has a condition." (= 可以是任何不方便明说的疾病)
```

**社交行为的委婉说法：**
```
"She's... friendly." (= 性道德不严格)
"He keeps company with a certain type." (= 交友不慎)
"They have an understanding." (= 婚外情双方都知道)
"She's been seen about town with..." (= 公开约会/有染)
"He has certain... appetites." (= 性方面的暗示)
"We don't discuss the Hendersons." (= 他们家出了丑闻)
```

## 六、同一角色在不同场合的语域切换示例

### 6.1 律师的语域切换

```
# 法庭上：
"Your Honor, the prosecution's case rests on a single piece of circumstantial evidence.
I submit that this is wholly insufficient to establish guilt beyond a reasonable doubt."

# 办公室里对同事：
"Their case is garbage. One witness, and she's shaky. We've got this."

# 酒吧里对朋友：
"You know what? The son of a bitch probably did it. But that's not my problem.
My problem is getting him off, and I will."

# 家里对妻子：
"Long day, dear. I don't want to talk about work."
```

### 6.2 警探的语域切换

```
# 对上级汇报：
"Captain, the investigation has yielded several promising leads.
I expect to have the suspect in custody within forty-eight hours."

# 审讯室：
"I know you were there, Tommy. I know you saw what happened.
Now, you can tell me, or you can tell the DA. Your choice."

# 对妻子：
"It was a rough one today. Kid, maybe fifteen years old.
I don't want to talk about it."

# 对线人（street level）：
"What do you got for me? And it better be good — I'm in no mood."

# 对黑帮（如果腐败）：
"The heat's on, Mr. Torrio. I can keep it off your boys, but it's gonna cost more this month."
```

### 6.3 俱乐部歌手的语域切换

```
# 舞台上（表演语域）：
"Good evening, ladies and gentlemen. I'd like to dedicate this next number
to all the lovers in the room tonight..."

# 后台对经理：
"I'm not going on until you pay me what you owe me. Two weeks, Eddie. TWO WEEKS."

# 对追求者（利用魅力）：
"You're sweet. Buy me a drink and maybe I'll sing something just for you."

# 对闺蜜（完全真实）：
"My feet are killing me, my voice is shot, and the piano player
was three drinks deep before the second set. I need a new life."
```

### 6.4 意大利裔餐馆老板的语域切换

```
# 对美国客人：
"Welcome, welcome! Best Italian food in Chicago. Sit, sit. I bring you the menu."

# 对意大利裔老朋友：
"Eh, compare! Come stai? Siediti, mangia. I make your favorite."

# 对供应商（谈判）：
"This tomato is no good. I no pay full price for bad tomato."

# 对家人（纯意大利语或混合）：
"Maria! Vieni qui. I need help in the kitchen. Andiamo, andiamo."

# 面对侦探（防御性）：
"I don't know nothing about no murder, sir. I'm just a cook.
I make pasta, I serve the wine — I mean, the soda. We no serve wine. Is against the law."
```

## 七、特殊沟通场景的语言规则

### 7.1 电话通话（1920s 的新媒介）

1920s 的电话通话有特殊的语言规范（电话仍然是相对新鲜的技术）：

```
# 正式接听：
"Hello? Henderson residence."
"Good afternoon, Whitmore and Associates."
"Palmer House Hotel, how may I direct your call?"

# 非正式接听：
"Hello?" / "Yeah?" / "Who's this?"

# 通话质量问题经常影响对话：
"Hello? HELLO? Can you hear me? Speak up!"
"The line's bad. Say that again?"
"Operator? OPERATOR? I've been disconnected."

# 挂电话：
"Good day." (正式)
"I'll call again tomorrow." (中性)
(直接挂断——表示愤怒或紧急)

# 电话中的隐私焦虑（线路可能被监听/接线员可能在听）：
"I can't discuss this over the telephone."
"Meet me at the usual place. I'll explain in person."
"You never know who's listening on these party lines."
```

### 7.2 书面通信 vs 口头语言

1920s 的书信语言比口语正式得多（这个差距比现代大得多）：

```
# 商务信函开头：
"Dear Sir: I write to you regarding the matter of..."
"Dear Madam: It has come to my attention that..."
"To Whom It May Concern:"

# 私人信函：
"My dearest Helen, I hope this letter finds you well..."
"Dear Tom, I'm writing with news that cannot wait..."
"Mother, forgive me for not writing sooner..."

# 匿名威胁信（侦探游戏常见道具）：
"I KNOW WHAT YOU DID. PAY UP OR ELSE."
"YOU HAVE BEEN WARNED. DO NOT GO TO THE POLICE."
"If you value your life, leave Chicago by Friday."
# 注意：匿名信的用词/拼写错误可以暗示写信人的教育水平和族裔背景
```

### 7.3 报纸语言（角色可能引用或阅读报纸）

```
# 标题风格（1920s 报纸标题更戏剧化）：
"GANGLAND SLAYING ON NORTH SIDE — POLICE BAFFLED"
"BOOTLEG KING ARRESTED IN MIDNIGHT RAID"
"SOCIETY MATRON FOUND DEAD IN LAKE SHORE MANSION"
"MYSTERY DEEPENS IN HENDERSON MURDER CASE"

# 报道正文（更正式的新闻体）：
"Police are at a loss to explain the circumstances surrounding the death of..."
"According to sources close to the investigation..."
"The victim, identified as Mr. Harold Henderson, aged 47, of Lake Shore Drive..."
"Authorities have declined to comment on reports that..."
```

## 八、语域错位作为叙事工具

### 8.1 语域错位暴露身份

当角色使用了不符合其场合/身份的语域时，就是推理线索：

```
# "仆人"用了上流社会的词汇：
Butler: "The provenance of that artifact is — I mean — I wouldn't know about such things, sir."
Detective: (注意到 "provenance" 这个词)

# "绅士"在压力下用了底层语言：
Gentleman: "This is preposterous, I — look, you can't pin this on me, see? I ain't — I'm not involved."
Detective: (注意到 "see?" 和 "ain't" 的滑出)

# "外国人"的英语突然太好了：
"Foreigner": "I am not understanding — I do not comprehend the implication of your inquiry."
Detective: ("comprehend" 和 "implication" 不是英语初学者的词汇)

# 正式场合中泄露了非正式关系：
At a party: "Mr. Henderson and I are merely business acquaintances."
Later, overheard: "Harry, you promised me you'd handle this!"
Detective: (公开叫 Mr. Henderson，私下叫 Harry)
```

### 8.2 故意的语域操控

老练的角色会故意切换语域来达到目的：

```
# 黑帮老大在不同场合的切换：
# 对市长（上流社会语域）：
"Your Honor, I'm merely a businessman who takes an interest in civic affairs."
# 对手下（街头语域）：
"If that rat opens his mouth one more time, shut it. Permanently."
# 对侦探（模糊语域——试探对方）：
"Detective Brennan, is it? I've heard good things. Sit down. Let's talk like reasonable men."

# 侦探利用语域切换获取信息：
# 对上流社会（模仿其语域以获得信任）：
"I appreciate your time, Mrs. Whitmore. I understand how distressing this must be."
# 对工人阶级（切换到平等语域以建立信任）：
"Look, pal, I'm not here to cause trouble. I just need to know what you saw."
# 对犯罪分子（使用其语域以展示自己不好骗）：
"Cut the crap, Vinnie. I know you were there. So let's skip the song and dance."
```
