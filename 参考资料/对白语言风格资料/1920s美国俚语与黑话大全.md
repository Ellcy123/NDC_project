## 一、禁酒令相关俚语

禁酒令（Prohibition, 1920-1933）催生了 1920s 最具特色的一批俚语。这些词在当时几乎人人都懂，但使用频率因阶层和场合而异。

### 1.1 场所相关

| 俚语 | 含义 | 例句 | 语境说明 |
|------|------|------|---------|
| **speakeasy** | 地下酒吧（非法售酒场所） | "I know a speakeasy on Clark Street. Real classy joint." | 各阶层通用。上流社会可能说 "a private club" |
| **blind pig / blind tiger** | 低端地下酒吧 | "That blind pig on Halsted got raided last week." | 比 speakeasy 更底层的场所 |
| **juice joint** | 卖酒的地方 | "Every other basement on this block is a juice joint." | 工人阶级用语 |
| **gin mill** | 廉价酒吧 | "He spends every paycheck at the gin mill." | 带贬义 |
| **clip joint** | 宰客酒吧（价格离谱） | "Don't go there — it's a clip joint. Five bucks for rotgut." | 警告性用语 |

### 1.2 酒水相关

| 俚语 | 含义 | 例句 | 语境说明 |
|------|------|------|---------|
| **hooch** | 私酒（泛指非法酒精） | "Got any hooch? I'm dying of thirst over here." | 最通用的私酒称呼 |
| **bathtub gin** | 自制劣质杜松子酒 | "Careful with that bathtub gin. Last batch blinded a fella." | 字面意思——在浴缸里酿的 |
| **giggle water** | 酒（幽默说法） | "Pour me some of that giggle water, would you?" | 轻松场合，女性也常用 |
| **coffin varnish** | 极劣质的私酒 | "This stuff tastes like coffin varnish." | 用于嘲笑酒的品质 |
| **panther piss / panther sweat** | 极难喝的烈酒 | "What is this, panther piss? A man could go blind." | 粗俗，限工人阶级/男性 |
| **the real McCoy** | 正品走私酒（非本地自酿） | "This is the real McCoy — straight from Canada." | 来源：走私商 Bill McCoy 以品质闻名 |
| **rotgut** | 劣质酒 | "You call this whiskey? It's rotgut." | 通用贬义词 |
| **needle beer** | 加了工业酒精的近似啤酒 | "All they got is needle beer. Better than nothing." | 工人阶级 |
| **jake** | 牙买加姜汁（含酒精的"药用"饮品） | "He's been drinking jake. That stuff'll paralyze you." | jake leg（jake 中毒导致的瘫痪）是真实社会问题 |

### 1.3 走私/贩酒相关

| 俚语 | 含义 | 例句 | 语境说明 |
|------|------|------|---------|
| **bootlegger** | 私酒贩子 | "Every bootlegger in Chicago answers to Capone." | 通用 |
| **rum runner** | 走私酒的人（尤指跨境） | "Rum runners bring it across from Canada, down the lake." | 特指水路走私 |
| **to bootleg** | 贩卖私酒（动词） | "Half the cops in this precinct are bootlegging on the side." | |
| **on the wagon** | 戒酒 | "He's been on the wagon since Easter. Won't last." | 禁酒令前就有，但此时更讽刺 |
| **off the wagon** | 戒酒失败/重新喝酒 | "Fell off the wagon last Friday. Hard." | |
| **hip flask** | 随身携带的扁酒瓶 | "Every man at that party had a hip flask." | 上流社会也用——禁酒令让饮酒成了叛逆时尚 |

### 1.4 醉酒程度词汇（从微醺到烂醉，1920s 有极丰富的表达）

```
轻度：tipsy, feeling good, half-seas over, lit, lit up
中度：plastered, oiled, lubricated, tight, pickled, soused, stewed
重度：blotto, fried, embalmed, stiff, paralyzed, ossified, zozzled
烂醉：blind drunk, dead drunk, stinking drunk, three sheets to the wind
宿醉：have a hangover, feeling rough, "the morning after"

例句：
"He was absolutely blotto by nine o'clock."
"She's a little lit tonight — two cocktails and she's dancing on tables."
"I got so ossified last night I don't remember getting home."
"He's three sheets to the wind. Somebody call him a cab."
```

## 二、爵士乐 / 音乐圈俚语

爵士乐在 1920s 从黑人社区走向主流，带来了一整套俚语，很多至今仍在使用。

| 俚语 | 含义 | 例句 | 语境说明 |
|------|------|------|---------|
| **cat** | 爵士乐手/酷的人 | "He's a real cat — plays trumpet like nobody else." | 来源于非裔美国人俚语 |
| **hep / hip** | 时髦的、懂行的 | "Are you hep to the new sound?" | hep 是 1920s 写法，hip 到 1930s-40s 才更常见 |
| **square** | 不时髦的人、古板的人 | "Don't be such a square. Live a little." | 音乐圈/年轻人用 |
| **hotsy-totsy** | 非常好、完美 | "Everything's hotsy-totsy, baby." | 轻浮语气 |
| **the bee's knees** | 极好的 | "That trumpet solo was the bee's knees." | 1920s 最经典的赞美俚语之一 |
| **the cat's meow** | 极好的（同上） | "Have you tried the new cocktail? It's the cat's meow." | 与 bee's knees 同义 |
| **the cat's pajamas** | 极好的（同上） | "She thinks she's the cat's pajamas." | 可带轻微讽刺（自以为了不起） |
| **jazz** (动词) | 使兴奋/加速/胡闹 | "Don't jazz me — tell it straight." | 1920s 时 jazz 作动词含义更广泛 |
| **jive** | 胡说/欺骗/爵士风格的 | "Don't give me that jive." | 偏非裔美国人社区 |
| **cooking** | 演奏得很棒 | "The band's really cooking tonight." | 音乐圈用语 |
| **gig** | 演出/工作 | "I got a gig at the Sunset Café." | 音乐圈，后扩展到一般工作 |

## 三、犯罪 / 黑帮用语

1920s 芝加哥是有组织犯罪的中心（Capone, Torrio, Moran, O'Banion），黑帮用语渗透到了日常语言中。

### 3.1 人物称谓

| 俚语 | 含义 | 例句 |
|------|------|------|
| **big shot / big cheese** | 大人物、老大 | "He thinks he's a big shot just because he knows Capone." |
| **boss** | 老大 | "The boss says nobody talks to the cops." |
| **moll** | 黑帮成员的女友 | "She's Vinnie's moll. Don't even look at her." |
| **torpedo** | 杀手/打手 | "They sent a torpedo after him. He's lucky to be alive." |
| **goon / gorilla** | 打手/保镖 | "He's got two goons with him everywhere he goes." |
| **stool pigeon / snitch / rat** | 告密者 | "Somebody ratted. There's a stool pigeon in the outfit." |
| **fence** | 销赃者 | "Take the jewels to Morrie. He's the best fence in town." |
| **grifter** | 骗子 | "He's a small-time grifter. Nothing big." |
| **yegg** | 保险柜盗贼 | "They brought in a yegg from Detroit to crack the safe." |
| **mug** | 傻瓜/笨蛋 | "What kind of mug leaves fingerprints at the scene?" |
| **palooka** | 笨蛋/二流拳手 | "Send that palooka in. I want a word." |
| **shyster** | 不正当的律师 | "Get me a shyster. The best one money can buy." |

### 3.2 动作/行为

| 俚语 | 含义 | 例句 |
|------|------|------|
| **bump off / rub out** | 杀掉 | "They bumped off Joey last Thursday." |
| **put on the spot** | 设计杀某人 | "Somebody put him on the spot. It was a setup." |
| **take for a ride** | 带出去杀掉（字面：开车带走） | "They took him for a ride. Found him in the river." |
| **plug** | 开枪打 | "Plug him if he moves." |
| **drill** | 开枪杀 | "They drilled him full of holes." |
| **knock off** | 抢劫/杀掉 | "They knocked off the First National Bank." |
| **pinch** | 逮捕 | "The cops pinched three guys last night." |
| **spring** | 保释出来 | "His lawyer sprung him by morning." |
| **finger** | 指认/出卖 | "Who fingered me? I want a name." |
| **muscle in** | 强行介入 | "Capone's boys are muscling in on the North Side." |
| **shake down** | 敲诈勒索 | "The cops shake down every shop on this block." |
| **case the joint** | 踩点（为犯罪做准备） | "I cased the joint. Two exits, one guard." |
| **lay low / lie low** | 躲起来 | "Lay low for a while. The heat's on." |
| **skip town** | 逃离城市 | "He skipped town before the cops could nab him." |
| **go straight** | 金盆洗手 | "I'm going straight. No more rackets." |

### 3.3 金钱与赃物

| 俚语 | 含义 | 例句 |
|------|------|------|
| **racket** | 非法生意/骗局 | "Bootlegging's the biggest racket in town." |
| **take** | 赃款/收入 | "What was the take? ... Five grand? Not bad." |
| **cut** | 分成 | "Everybody gets their cut. That's how it works." |
| **on the take** | 受贿 | "Half the department's on the take." |
| **grease** | 贿赂 | "You gotta grease the right palms in this town." |
| **hot** | 被通缉的/赃物 | "Don't touch that — it's hot merchandise." |
| **heist** | 抢劫（名词/动词） | "The jewelry heist on Michigan Avenue? That was us." |

### 3.4 处境/状态

| 俚语 | 含义 | 例句 |
|------|------|------|
| **on the lam** | 在逃 | "He's been on the lam since the shooting." |
| **the heat** | 警方压力/搜查 | "The heat's on. Everybody cool it." |
| **in the hot seat** | 处境危险 | "You're in the hot seat now, pal." |
| **up the river** | 进监狱 | "He's going up the river for ten years." |
| **doing a stretch** | 服刑 | "He's doing a five-year stretch at Joliet." |
| **the big house** | 监狱 | "One more pinch and it's the big house for you." |
| **sing** | 向警察供出同伙 | "If he sings, we're all done for." |
| **clam up** | 闭嘴/拒绝说话 | "He clammed up the second the cops walked in." |
| **take the rap** | 替人顶罪 | "Somebody's gotta take the rap. It ain't gonna be me." |

## 七、其他高频表达

### 7.1 惊叹 / 感叹

```
"Holy smokes!"
"Holy mackerel!"
"Jumpin' Jehoshaphat!"
"Well, I'll be..."
"Well, I'll be damned." (粗俗)
"For crying out loud!"
"For the love of Pete!"
"For Pete's sake!"
"What in the Sam Hill...?"
"Land sakes!"
"Cheese it!" (= 小心！/警察来了！)
"Jeepers!" / "Jeepers creepers!"
"Gee whiz!"
"Oh, applesauce!" (= 哎呀！/胡说！)
```

### 7.2 肯定/同意

```
"You said it!" (= 说得对)
"You bet!" / "You betcha!" (= 当然)
"And how!" (= 非常同意)
"I'll say!" (= 我完全同意)
"Attaboy!" / "Attagirl!" (= 好样的)
"Now you're on the trolley!" (= 现在你懂了)
"That's the ticket!" (= 就是这样)
"Swell!" (= 太好了)
"Solid." (= 好的/同意)
```

### 7.3 否定/不信

```
"Nuts!" (= 胡说/才不)
"Nuts to you!" (= 去你的)
"Says you!" (= 你说了算？才不)
"Sez who?" (= 谁说的？)
"Oh yeah?" (= 是吗？我不信)
"Banana oil!" (= 胡扯)
"Don't take any wooden nickels." (= 别上当)
"I wasn't born yesterday." (= 我不是傻子)
"Tell me another one." (= 我才不信)
"Pull the other one." (= 别骗我)
```

### 7.4 通用口头禅

```
"See?" / "...see?" (句尾语气词，极常用于 1920s)
"Get me?" (= 明白吗？)
"Savvy?" (= 懂了？)
"...and that's that." (= 就这样，没得商量)
"What's the big idea?" (= 你什么意思？/搞什么鬼？)
"What's eating you?" (= 你怎么了？)
"So what?" (= 那又怎样？)
"Search me." (= 我不知道)
"Beats me." (= 我也不懂)
"Don't know from nothing." (= 什么都不知道)
```

## 八、俚语使用的阶级/场合禁忌

### 8.1 各阶层的俚语使用频率

| 阶层 | 俚语使用程度 | 常用类别 | 绝不会用 |
|------|-------------|---------|---------|
| Old Money | 极少 | 偶尔用讽刺性俚语 | 犯罪黑话、粗俗俚语 |
| New Money | 中等 | 流行俚语（显得时髦） | 移民英语、底层黑话 |
| 中产 | 中等 | 日常俚语、商业俚语 | 犯罪黑话（除非特殊场合） |
| 工人 | 高 | 全部类别 | 法律拉丁语、上流社会委婉语 |
| 犯罪圈 | 极高 | 犯罪黑话为主 | 正式用语 |
| 新移民 | 低 | 学到的常见俚语 | 大部分——词汇量限制 |

### 8.2 性别差异

1920s 的俚语使用有明显性别区分：
