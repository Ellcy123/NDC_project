# 阶级与身份语言差异 — 1920s 美国/芝加哥

> 本文档为 NDC 项目（1925 年芝加哥背景）对白撰写参考资料。
> 核心原则：**阶级通过语言外显**——词汇选择、句法复杂度、发音暗示、礼仪用语都是角色身份的标记。

---

## 一、上流社会 / 富人阶层

### 1.1 Old Money（老钱家族）

Old Money 指在美国已传承两三代以上的财富家族（如芝加哥的 Palmer、McCormick、Armour 家族）。其语言风格深受东海岸精英教育（prep school + Ivy League）和英式英语传统的影响。

**核心语言特征：**

- **刻意的低调（understated elegance）**：避免直接谈论金钱、价格、财富。用词精确但不炫耀。
- **长句、从句嵌套**：受古典教育影响，句式复杂，逻辑层次分明。
- **拉丁语/法语借词自然使用**：不是炫耀，而是教育背景的自然流露。
- **被动语态偏好**："It has been arranged" 而非 "I arranged it"。
- **间接表达**：不说 "No"，说 "I'm afraid that wouldn't be advisable."

**典型用语与例句：**

| 场景 | Old Money 说法 | 对比（普通说法） |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| 拒绝邀请 | "I'm terribly sorry, but I find myself otherwise engaged that evening." | "Can't make it." |
| 评价食物 | "The consommé is rather agreeable, wouldn't you say?" | "Good soup." |
| 谈论金钱 | "One prefers not to discuss matters of commerce at table." | "Let's not talk money." |
| 表达不满 | "I confess I find the arrangement somewhat... irregular." | "I don't like it." |
| 介绍自己 | "How do you do. I'm Edward Whitmore. My family has been in Lake Forest since the eighties." | "I'm Ed. I'm from up north." |
| 命令下人 | "Would you be so kind as to see that the car is brought round?" | "Get the car." |

**句式范例（可直接用于对话设计）：**

```
"I daresay you'll find the matter has already been attended to."
"One doesn't simply appear at the Drake without a proper introduction."
"My dear fellow, I'm sure you mean well, but this really isn't the sort of thing one discusses openly."
"The family has always maintained a certain... discretion in these affairs."
"I should think it rather beneath us to concern ourselves with such trifles."
"Might I suggest we adjourn to the study? The help needn't hear everything."
```

**禁忌与回避：**
- 绝不直接说数字金额："a considerable sum" / "not an insignificant amount"
- 绝不用粗俗俚语（即使私下也克制）
- 绝不承认自己不知道某个文化知识点——会用模糊语搪塞
- 称呼永远正式：Mr./Mrs./Miss + 姓氏，即使认识多年
- 避免提及"工作"——用 "affairs" / "interests" / "responsibilities" 替代

### 1.2 New Money（新钱暴发户）

1920s 的 New Money 主要来源于禁酒令走私、房地产投机、股市暴利、工业扩张。芝加哥尤其盛产靠肉类加工、铁路、禁酒令暴富的新贵。

**核心语言特征：**

- **过度使用大词**：为彰显教养，但常用错或用得不自然。
- **夸张的礼貌用语**：学 Old Money 的腔调但过了头。
- **频繁提及价格和品牌**：与 Old Money 的回避形成鲜明对比。
- **混合风格**：正式场合努力表现，但压力下或情绪激动时会滑入原来的阶层语言。
- **名词堆砌炫耀**：喜欢报出地名、品牌名、名人名字来证明自己的社交圈。

**典型用语与例句：**

```
"You should see my new Duesenberg — set me back twelve thousand, but it's worth every penny."
"We're having the Petersons over Saturday. You know the Petersons? Harold Peterson, from Peterson Steel?"
"I got this suit tailored on Fifth Avenue, New York. Only the best."
"Allow me to — what's the word — facilitate the introduction."
"My wife, she's taking French lessons now. Très bon, she says. Very cultured."
```

**New Money 的语言"穿帮"时刻：**

```
# 努力表现时：
"It would be my utmost pleasure to extend to you an invitation..."

# 压力下露馅时：
"Listen here, pal, I didn't claw my way up from the stockyards to take lip from some—"

# 用错大词时：
"The event was positively ostentatious." (想说 ostentatious 但该词本身就意味炫耀)
"I find the whole affair rather inconsequential." (想表达"重要"但用了反义词)
```

**New Money vs Old Money 的微妙冲突例句：**

```
New Money: "That painting cost me three thousand dollars."
Old Money: "How... forthcoming of you to share that."

New Money: "I know everybody who's anybody in this town."
Old Money: "Indeed. How very... democratic of you."

New Money: "We just bought a summer place in Lake Geneva."
Old Money: "Ah. The lake has become quite popular lately, hasn't it."
```

## 二、中产阶级

### 2.1 商人 / 店主 / 小业主

1920s 芝加哥的中产阶级包括：小店主、中层管理者、会计师、保险经纪人、小型工厂主。他们的语言特征介于正式与口语之间。

**核心语言特征：**

- **实用主义语言**：简明扼要，注重效率，不像上流社会那样绕弯子。
- **商业术语自然融入日常**："bottom line," "square deal," "on the level"
- **适度的俚语使用**：会用流行俚语但不过分粗俗。
- **礼貌但不卑不亢**：对上不谄媚，对下不粗暴。
- **注重信誉相关的表达**："a man of his word," "good for it," "on the square"

**典型用语与例句：**

```
"I run a clean business, Detective. Always have."
"Look, I'll give it to you straight — the books don't add up."
"A deal's a deal. I shook on it, didn't I?"
"Business has been slow since the crash — well, not crash, but the slowdown."
"I'm not looking for trouble. I just want what's fair."
"My customers trust me. That's worth more than money."
"I can vouch for him — known him going on ten years."
```

**面对权威（警察/侦探）时：**

```
"I've got nothing to hide, but I'd appreciate it if you kept this quiet. Bad for business."
"If you've got a warrant, I'll cooperate. If not, I've got a shop to run."
"I pay my taxes, I follow the law — mostly — and I mind my own business."
```

### 2.2 白领职员 / 中层管理

**典型用语与例句：**

```
"The boss wants the report by Friday. That's just how it is."
"I keep my head down and do my job. Office politics isn't my game."
"Between you and me, something's not right in accounting."
"I'm not paid enough to ask those kinds of questions."
"He's a company man through and through."
```

## 三、工人阶级

### 3.1 总体语言特征

1920s 芝加哥的工人阶级包括：肉类加工厂工人、钢铁工人、码头工人、建筑工人、铁路工人、出租车司机、服务员、清洁工等。

**核心语言特征：**

- **短句为主**：很少使用从句嵌套，直奔主题。
- **双重否定常见**："I don't know nothing about that." / "He ain't never been here."
- **非标准语法自然出现**："He done it." / "She seen him." / "We was there."
- **大量俚语和行话**：每个行业有自己的术语。
- **身体语言补充表达**：耸肩、吐口水、用下巴指方向——这些在对话描写中应体现。
- **粗话更频繁**：但 1920s 的粗话标准与现代不同（见下文）。
- **直接、不修饰**：不会用委婉语绕弯子。

**典型用语与例句：**

```
"I been working the yards since I was fourteen. Don't tell me about hard work."
"The foreman's a real son of a bitch, pardon my French."
"You want answers? Buy me a drink first."
"I ain't seen nothing, I ain't heard nothing, and I ain't saying nothing."
"A guy's gotta eat. You do what you gotta do."
"He's alright, that fella. Wouldn't steer you wrong."
"The missus is gonna have my hide if I'm late again."
```

### 3.2 肉类加工厂工人（Union Stock Yards 特色）

芝加哥的 Union Stock Yards 是世界最大的肉类加工中心，雇佣数万工人。这里的语言有独特的行业色彩。

```
"Another day on the killing floor. Ain't nothing pretty about it."
"You get used to the smell. After a while, you don't even notice."
"The union says we're getting a raise. I'll believe it when I see it."
"Packingtown — that's what they call our neighborhood. Real fancy, huh?"
"I handle the hogs. My brother does cattle. Pop did cattle too, before his back gave out."
```

### 3.3 码头工人 / 铁路工人

```
"Load came in light today. Boss ain't gonna be happy."
"You lift with your legs, kid, or you ain't gonna have a back by thirty."
"The rails don't care if it's Christmas. Freight's gotta move."
"I pulled a double yesterday. Sixteen hours. My feet are killing me."
```

### 3.4 出租车司机 / 服务业

```
"Where to, Mac?"
"Hop in. I know a shortcut."
"That'll be thirty-five cents, and a tip never hurt nobody."
"You want the Palmer House? Sure thing. Fancy place for a fancy fella."
"I drive all kinds — bankers, bootleggers, you name it. I don't ask questions."
```

### 3.5 工人阶级的1920s特有粗话与脏话

1920s 的粗话标准与现代差异很大。以下是当时常见但被视为粗俗的表达：

```
"Hell" / "Damn" / "Goddamn" — 1920s 已经很强烈，在体面场合不可接受
"Son of a bitch" — 极度粗俗，通常加 "pardon my French"
"Bastard" — 非常严重的侮辱
"Horse's ass" — 骂人蠢
"Go to hell" — 相当于现代的极端粗话
"What the devil" — "What the hell" 的稍文明版
"Balderdash" / "Poppycock" / "Hogwash" — 表达"胡说八道"的不同粗俗程度
"Nuts to you" — 表达不屑/拒绝
```

## 四、赤贫 / 新移民

### 4.1 总体语言特征

1920s 芝加哥是移民城市，各族裔聚居区有鲜明的语言特征。新移民的英语通常表现出：

- **有限词汇量**：用简单词汇表达复杂意思
- **母语语法干扰**：词序、时态、冠词等方面的系统性错误
- **code-switching**：英语中夹杂母语词汇（特别是情绪激动时）
- **过度礼貌**：不确定文化规则时倾向于过度正式
- **同族裔间用母语，对外用蹩脚英语**

### 4.2 意大利移民的英语特征

意大利移民主要聚居在 Near West Side（Little Italy 区域）。

**常见语法错误模式：**
- 冠词省略或错用："I go to store" / "I have the hunger"
- 双重元音化和尾音添加：为英语单词加元音尾
- 动词时态简化：常用现在时替代过去时
- 代词混淆：he/she 混用（意大利语中 lui/lei 不同但有的方言区分不明显）

**典型例句：**

```
"My boy, he work hard. Every day, he work."
"In the old country, we no have this problem."
"Please, signore, I no want trouble."
"The bread, she is good today, no?"
"I tell you the truth — I swear on my mother."
"We come here for the better life. America, she promise, no?"
```

### 4.3 波兰移民的英语特征

波兰移民聚居在西北区（Polish Triangle / Wicker Park 一带），形成了全美最大的波兰社区之一。

**常见语法错误模式：**
- 冠词几乎完全缺失（波兰语无冠词）："I go to factory" / "He is good man"
- 时态标记弱：用副词而非动词变化表达时间 "Yesterday I go to work"
- 介词错用："I am on the work" / "I go by the street"
- th 发音困难：发成 /d/ 或 /t/，"the" → "de"，"think" → "tink"

**典型例句：**

```
"I work in factory, stockyard. Is hard work but is honest."
"My wife, she speak no English. Only Polish."
"Please, I no understand. You speak slow?"
"In Poland, was very bad. Here, is better. We work, we eat."
"I come five year ago. Still I learn the English."
"De boss, he say come early tomorrow. So I come."
```

### 4.4 爱尔兰移民（第二三代）的英语特征

到 1920s，爱尔兰裔已经是第二三代了，大多英语流利，但爱尔兰英语特征仍然明显，尤其在 Bridgeport、Back of the Yards 等工人阶级社区。

**语言特征：**
- Hiberno-English 句式遗留："I'm after seeing him"（= I just saw him）
- 强调用法："It's himself that did it" / "'Tis a grand thing"
- 语气词：Sure, Now, So, Well now
- 比喻和诅咒的丰富性：爱尔兰人以修辞能力著称
- 较少语法错误（英语已是母语），但有鲜明的节奏和表达习惯

**典型例句：**

```
"Sure and isn't it a fine mess we're in now."
"I'm after telling you twice already — the man was nowhere to be seen."
"God between us and all harm, but that's a terrible thing."
"Would you look at the state of him. Jaysus."
"Ah now, don't be giving me that look. I've done nothing wrong, so I haven't."
"The drink'll be the death of him, mark my words."
"He's a fierce stubborn man, fierce stubborn altogether."
```

### 4.5 犹太移民的英语特征

东欧犹太移民主要聚居在 Maxwell Street 一带（Near West Side），到 1920s 部分已迁往 Lawndale。

**语言特征：**
- Yiddish 词汇自然嵌入：mensch, chutzpah, schmuck, meshuggeneh, oy vey
- 反问句式频繁："So what am I, chopped liver?" / "You call this a deal?"
- 倒装强调句：把宾语或状语提前 "A doctor, he wants to be." / "Money, he's got plenty."
- 丰富的谚语和比喻
- 幽默和自嘲作为社交工具

**典型例句：**

```
"So he says to me, he says, 'Take it or leave it.' Some deal, huh?"
"A man works his whole life, and for what? So his son can be a bum?"
"Oy, don't get me started. You want I should talk all night?"
"He's a mensch, that one. A real mensch. Not like his brother."
"What do I know from politics? I sell shoes."
"Such a tumult over nothing. The world is going meshuggeneh."
"Listen, bubbeleh, in this neighborhood, you mind your own business."
```

## 五、阶级间对话的权力动态

### 5.1 上对下（富人对穷人/仆人）

```
# 善意但居高临下：
"You've done well for yourself, considering your circumstances."
"I'm sure you're doing the best you can with what you have."

# 直接命令（Old Money 风格，表面礼貌）：
"I wonder if you might attend to the matter we discussed."
"I trust you'll see to it that this doesn't happen again."

# 直接命令（New Money 风格，更赤裸）：
"You work for me, pal. Don't forget that."
"I didn't hire you to think. I hired you to do what I tell you."
```

### 5.2 下对上（穷人/仆人对富人）

```
# 恭顺：
"Yes, sir. Right away, sir."
"Whatever you say, sir. You're the boss."

# 压抑的不满：
"Of course, sir. Will there be anything else, sir?" (语气暗含讽刺)
"I wouldn't presume to say, sir."

# 背后议论：
"Those Lake Shore Drive types think they own the world. Well, maybe they do."
"She's got more money than God and less sense than a chicken."
```

### 5.3 同阶级内部的微妙差异

即使同属工人阶级，也有内部等级：

```
# 技术工人看不起非技术工人：
"I'm a machinist, pal. Skilled trade. Don't lump me in with the sweepers."

# 本地出生的移民后代看不起新移民：
"My old man came over in '90. We're Americans now. These new ones, fresh off the boat..."

# 有工作的看不起流浪汉：
"At least I earn my keep. Not like those bums on Madison Street."
```

## 六、语言切换的戏剧性应用

### 6.1 角色在压力下的阶级"滑落"

这是对话设计中最有价值的技巧：角色在情绪激动、惊恐、愤怒时，会不自觉地滑回自己真实阶层的语言习惯。

```
# 一个假装上流社会的暴发户在被揭穿时：
正常状态："I assure you, everything is perfectly in order."
被揭穿时："You listen here, you little — I came up from nothing, see? NOTHING."

# 一个假装工人阶级的卧底上流社会成员：
正常状态："Yeah, I been working the docks. Tough gig."
不小心时："I find that rather — I mean, that ain't right."

# 一个受过教育的黑帮成员：
正常状态（对手下）："Get it done. I don't wanna hear excuses."
正常状态（对上流社会）："I appreciate your hospitality, Mrs. Palmer. The evening has been delightful."
```

### 6.2 语言作为身份伪装的线索

在侦探推理游戏中，语言不一致是重要线索：

```
# 可疑的"仆人"说了不该知道的词：
"The provenance of that painting is — I mean, I dunno nothing about art, sir."

# 可疑的"绅士"用了工人阶级的表达：
"This whole setup is — I mean, this arrangement seems rather irregular."

# 可疑的"美国人"有外国腔的句式：
"I am here already twenty years." (德语语序影响)
```

## 七、服饰/外表与语言的关联（辅助对话设计）

角色的外表描写应与其语言风格一致，以下是参考：

| 阶级 | 服饰关键词 | 语言关键词 |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Old Money | 低调剪裁、暗色调、传承首饰 | 克制、间接、从句嵌套 |
| New Money | 华丽面料、大宝石、新潮款式 | 夸张、炫耀、用错大词 |
| 中产 | 整洁但不奢华、三件套西装 | 实用、直接、适度正式 |
| 工人 | 工装、帽子、磨损衣物 | 短句、俚语、双重否定 |
| 赤贫/移民 | 旧衣、不合身、混搭 | 语法错误、母语夹杂、过度礼貌 |
