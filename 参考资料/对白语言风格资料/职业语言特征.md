# 职业语言特征 — 1920s 美国/芝加哥

> 本文档为 NDC 项目（1925 年芝加哥背景）对白撰写参考资料。
> 核心原则：**职业塑造语言习惯**——一个人的工作会深刻影响他的词汇库、句式偏好、修辞策略和沟通目标。

---

## 一、律师

### 1.1 语言总体风格

1920s 的律师受古典修辞训练影响较深（Lincoln-Douglas 辩论传统尚在），语言风格比现代律师更具文学性和演说感。芝加哥的法律界同时受到实务主义影响，不如东海岸那么学院派。

**核心语言特征：**

- **精确性强迫症**：每个词都经过选择，避免模糊表达
- **对抗性修辞**：擅长用问题引导对方进入逻辑陷阱
- **法律拉丁语自然嵌入**：habeas corpus, prima facie, modus operandi, in absentia
- **条件句大量使用**："If it were the case that..." / "Assuming, arguendo..."
- **主动控制对话节奏**：用问句、停顿、重复来施加压力

**法庭正式用语：**

```
"Your Honor, the prosecution submits that..."
"I put it to the witness that on the night in question..."
"Objection, Your Honor. Counsel is leading the witness."
"Let the record show that the defendant was unable to answer."
"The evidence will demonstrate, beyond any reasonable doubt, that..."
"I move to strike the witness's last remark from the record."
"With the court's indulgence, I should like to redirect."
```

**办公室/私下用语（仍保持专业但更口语化）：**

```
"Off the record? He's guilty as sin."
"The prosecution's got nothing. Circumstantial, all of it."
"My client isn't talking. And I'd advise yours not to either."
"There's the law, and then there's what happens in this town. You know how it works."
"I've seen judges bought for less than the price of that suit."
"Get me the deposition by Thursday, or we're sunk."
"A man is entitled to a defense, no matter what you think of him."
```

**交叉询问技巧用语（对侦探角色最有价值）：**

```
"Let me make sure I understand you correctly..."
"And you're quite certain of that? Quite certain?"
"Isn't it true that on the night of March 14th, you were nowhere near the premises?"
"You testified just moments ago that — and I quote — you 'never met the man.' Do you stand by that?"
"A simple yes or no will suffice."
"I find it curious, don't you, that the only witness happens to be your business partner?"
"So your testimony is that you heard nothing. Saw nothing. Knew nothing. Is that correct?"
```

### 1.2 检察官 vs 辩护律师的语言差异

```
# 检察官（道德权威感）：
"The people demand justice. The evidence demands a conviction."
"This man — this cold, calculating individual — robbed a family of their father."

# 辩护律师（质疑与同情并用）：
"What the prosecution calls evidence, I call coincidence."
"My client is a family man, a churchgoer, a veteran. Is this the face of a killer?"
"Reasonable doubt — those two words are the bedrock of our justice system."
```

## 二、私家侦探 / 警探

### 2.1 私家侦探（Private Investigator / Private Dick）

1920s 的私家侦探是一个新兴职业，形象介于法律执行者和地下世界之间。他们的语言风格成了后来 hard-boiled 文学的基础。

**核心语言特征：**

- **简洁、干燥、讽刺**：省略不必要的词，观察锐利
- **比喻丰富但低调**：不像文学家那样华丽，是日常比喻
- **第一人称叙述感**：即使在对话中也常带有解说的语气
- **street smart 的口吻**：不是学院派，是经验派
- **对各阶层都能调整语言**：这是职业需要

**典型对话用语：**

```
"I'm just asking questions. That's what I do."
"The way I see it, somebody's not telling the truth. Question is, who."
"I've been in this business long enough to know when a story doesn't add up."
"You can talk to me, or you can talk to the police. Your choice."
"I'm not a cop. I don't care who you sleep with or what you drink."
"Somebody went through a lot of trouble to make this look like an accident."
"Follow the money. That's the first rule."
"Every lock's got a key. Every lie's got a crack."
```

**向客户汇报时：**

```
"Here's what I've got so far, and you're not going to like it."
"Your husband was at the Palmer House Tuesday night. He wasn't alone."
"I can give you facts. What you do with them is your business."
"I need another week. And another fifty dollars."
```

**面对威胁时：**

```
"You're not the first guy to point a gun at me, and you probably won't be the last."
"If you wanted to scare me, you should've sent somebody bigger."
"I've been punched by better men than you. It didn't stop me then either."
```

### 2.2 警探 / 警察（Chicago PD）

1920s 芝加哥警察以腐败闻名（与 Capone 等黑帮的勾结是公开秘密）。语言风格反映了威权、cynicism 和腐败文化的混合。

**核心语言特征：**

- **威权命令句**：短促、不容质疑
- **警察行话**：pinch（逮捕）、collar（抓人）、beat（巡逻区）、grift（小骗局）
- **cynical 幽默**：见多了黑暗面的疲惫调侃
- **威胁性暗示**：不直说但让你知道后果
- **官僚体语言**（写报告/对上级时）与街头语言（对嫌疑人/线人时）的巨大反差

**巡逻警察（beat cop）用语：**

```
"Move along, nothing to see here."
"You got papers? Let me see some identification."
"This is my beat. I know every face on this block."
"Break it up, break it up. Take it somewhere else."
"You want trouble? Keep talking."
"I'm gonna pretend I didn't see that."
```

**警探（detective）用语：**

```
"Where were you Tuesday night between eight and midnight?"
"Don't leave town. We may have more questions."
"I've heard a lot of stories in this room. Yours is one of the worst."
"We've got a witness. We've got evidence. What we don't have is your side of the story."
"Look, make it easy on yourself. Tell me what happened."
"Save the sob story for the judge."
"You're looking at ten to twenty. Unless you've got something to trade."
```

**腐败警察的暗示性语言：**

```
"Nice place you got here. Shame if something were to happen to it."
"I'm a reasonable man. I'm sure we can come to an understanding."
"Mr. Torrio appreciates cooperation. So do I."
"The evidence might... go missing. If the price is right."
"You didn't hear this from me, understand?"
"Some cases, they just go cold. Nobody's fault. It happens."
```

## 三、记者

### 3.1 语言总体风格

1920s 是美国新闻业的黄金时代。芝加哥尤其是新闻重镇（《Chicago Tribune》《Chicago Daily News》《Herald and Examiner》），竞争激烈，记者文化鲜明。

**核心语言特征：**

- **节奏快、信息密度高**：受打字机和电报影响，惜字如金
- **新闻体倒金字塔自然融入口语**：先说最重要的事
- **cynical wit**：记者圈的自嘲和黑色幽默
- **线人网络术语**："source," "tip," "lead," "on background," "off the record"
- **自我神化**：1920s 记者以"第四权"自居，语言中带有使命感
- **打字机/印刷术语口语化**：copy（稿件）、deadline、scoop、beat、lead

**编辑部用语：**

```
"I've got a lead on the Henderson case. Give me two days."
"Kill the headline. It's libelous."
"Who's your source? — Can't say. — Then it doesn't run."
"The Trib's got the story. We need to get ahead of it."
"Rewrite! I need five hundred words by four o'clock!"
"Hold the presses — we've got a new angle."
"That's front page. Above the fold."
"Stop the presses" (极端重大新闻时才说，1920s 真的会物理停止印刷机)
```

**采访中的用语：**

```
"Mind if I ask you a few questions? Strictly for the record."
"The public has a right to know, Alderman."
"I'm not here to make trouble. I'm here to get the facts."
"Care to comment on the allegations?"
"My readers want to know — where was the mayor when this happened?"
"Off the record? ... (等对方说完) Thanks. That's going in tomorrow's edition."
"I've got two sources saying the opposite. Someone's lying."
```

**记者之间的行话：**

```
"It's a puff piece. Editor wants it soft."
"Bury the lede and you're done in this business."
"She's a stringer for the Daily News. Good nose for a story."
"This whole town runs on graft. Everybody knows, nobody prints it."
"The crime beat'll either make you or break you."
"I've been scooped twice this week. The boss is livid."
"That story's got legs. Follow it."
```

**记者的cynical 世界观表达：**

```
"In this town, truth is whatever the man with the biggest wallet says it is."
"Print the legend, kid. Nobody wants the truth — it's too ugly."
"Every politician I've ever met is either a crook or a fool. Usually both."
"You want justice? Read a fairy tale. You want the news? Read my column."
```

## 四、俱乐部 / 酒店经理

### 4.1 语言总体风格

1920s 芝加哥的高端酒店（The Drake, Palmer House, The Blackstone）和 speakeasy 俱乐部经理是重要的叙事角色。他们的语言特征是**八面玲珑**——能跟各阶层打交道。

**核心语言特征：**

- **永远不说"不"**：用迂回方式拒绝或回避
- **称呼精确**：记住每位重要客人的姓名和偏好
- **双关和暗示**：特别是涉及非法活动（禁酒令下卖酒）时
- **危机管理语言**：出事时保持冷静，语言安抚
- **阶级切换能力**：对贵宾用上流社会语言，对下属用直接命令

**对贵宾客人的用语：**

```
"Good evening, Mr. Whitmore. Your usual table has been prepared."
"The gentleman's preferences have been noted. We aim to please."
"I trust everything is to your satisfaction? If there's anything at all..."
"We pride ourselves on discretion, sir. What happens within these walls..."
"May I suggest the private dining room? More... intimate."
"Allow me to personally attend to the matter."
```

**回避敏感问题时：**

```
"I'm afraid I wouldn't know anything about that, Detective."
"We see a great many faces here. I couldn't possibly remember them all."
"Our clientele values privacy, and we respect that."
"I'd love to help, truly, but my memory seems to be... failing me tonight."
"Perhaps you'd like to speak to the owner. I'm merely the manager."
"That's above my pay grade, I'm afraid."
```

**关于酒水的暗语（禁酒令下）：**

```
"Can I offer you some... refreshment?" (暗示非法酒水)
"We have an excellent selection of... beverages." (用语气暗示)
"The gentleman asked for something with a little more... character."
"I believe the special reserve is available this evening." (代指走私烈酒)
"Tea" — 暗语，指威士忌（装在茶杯里喝）
```

**对下属的用语（完全不同的语域）：**

```
"Get Table Six another round, and make it snappy."
"If I see one more dirty glass on the floor, somebody's getting canned."
"The Moretti party is here. I want everything perfect. PERFECT. Understand?"
"Who let that bum in? Get him out before Mr. Henderson sees."
```

## 五、厨师 / 体力劳动者

### 5.1 餐厅厨师

1920s 芝加哥的高档餐厅厨师很多是法裔或意裔，普通餐厅和食堂的厨师则来自各个背景。

**厨房用语特征：**
- **极度简短**：厨房噪音大，沟通必须快速精确
- **命令句为主**：没有请求，只有指令
- **法语术语（高档厨房）**：mise en place, sauté, flambé, à la carte
- **食材/烹饪行话**：86'd（用完了/取消了）、on the fly（加急）、in the weeds（忙不过来）

```
"Order up! Two steaks, one rare, one well!"
"Eighty-six the lobster — we're out!"
"Behind you! Hot pan, coming through!"
"Who burned the sauce? This is garbage. Start over."
"We're in the weeds, boys. Move faster."
"Fire Table Four! They've been waiting twenty minutes!"
"Taste this. More salt? ... Good. Send it."
"The chef says if you overcook one more steak, you're washing dishes."
```

### 5.2 建筑工人 / 体力劳动者

1920s 芝加哥正在经历建筑热潮（Tribune Tower 1925 年完工，Wrigley Building 1924 年完工），大量建筑工人。

```
"Hand me that wrench, will ya?"
"Watch your head — beam coming down!"
"The foreman says we're behind schedule. Big surprise."
"I been laying brick since before you were born, kid."
"Union meeting tonight. You better show up if you know what's good for you."
"This whole building's gonna stand a hundred years. Because we built it right."
"My back's shot, my hands are raw, and I got three kids to feed. So yeah, I'll take the overtime."
```

## 六、歌手 / 表演者

### 6.1 语言总体风格

1920s 芝加哥的娱乐界以爵士乐和 vaudeville 为核心。South Side 的 Bronzeville 区是爵士乐圣地（The Sunset Café, The Plantation Café）。表演者语言融合了艺术圈的自恋、夜生活的放纵和 show business 的精明。

**核心语言特征：**

- **爵士乐术语融入日常表达**：hip, square, cat, solid, groovy (已在使用但尚未流行)
- **戏剧化的自我表达**：一切都更大、更强烈
- **自我中心的修辞**：常把话题转回自己
- **夜生活时间感**："早上"指中午，"晚上"指凌晨
- **对"正常人"的轻蔑**：称他们 "squares" / "civilians"

**歌手/音乐家的典型用语：**

```
"I don't sing for the money, darling. I sing because I must."
"The crowd was dead tonight. Couldn't get them going."
"I'm between engagements at the moment." (失业的体面说法)
"That new trumpet player? He's the real deal. Blows like nobody's business."
"The manager's a crook, but what else is new in this business?"
"I need a drink before I go on. Nerves, you know."
"You want to hear me sing, you come to the club. I don't do private shows. Well... not usually."
```

**后台/同行之间：**

```
"Who's headlining tonight? ... Her? She can't carry a tune in a bucket."
"The band's cooking tonight. Listen to that rhythm section."
"I'm telling you, talking pictures are going to change everything."
"This joint's got good acoustics, but the piano's out of tune."
"I used to play the big rooms in New York. Now look at me — a gin joint in Chicago."
```

**vaudeville 表演者用语：**

```
"Never follow a dog act. Nobody can top a dog act."
"Died out there tonight. Absolutely died. Not a single laugh."
"You gotta read the room, kid. Every crowd's different."
"It's all about the timing. Comedy is timing."
"The hook — they actually gave me the hook last week. Can you believe it?"
```

## 七、警察 / 公职人员

### 7.1 公职人员 / 政客

1920s 芝加哥的政治文化由 machine politics 主导（Big Bill Thompson 市长时期），腐败与裙带关系公开化。

**核心语言特征：**

- **官腔（official-speak）**：冗长、模糊、推卸责任
- **选举语言**：煽动性、承诺性、民粹主义
- **私下语言完全不同**：交易性、直白、威胁性
- **euphemism 大师**："contributions"（贿赂）、"arrangements"（交易）、"friends"（同党）

**公开场合（演讲/记者会）：**

```
"The good people of Chicago deserve better, and I intend to deliver."
"This administration has always stood for law and order."
"These allegations are politically motivated, and I will not dignify them with a response."
"I have the utmost confidence in our police department."
"Chicago is the greatest city in the greatest country on God's green earth."
```

**私下交易：**

```
"You scratch my back, I'll scratch yours. That's how this town works."
"The alderman needs a favor. You help him, he helps you. Simple."
"That judge? He's one of ours. Don't worry about the verdict."
"The ward's taken care of. Every precinct captain knows who to vote for."
"Listen, in this city, you're either with us or you're against us. There's no in-between."
"Forget what you saw. Trust me, it's better for everyone."
```

### 7.2 法官

```
"Order in the court. I will have order."
"Counsel, you are trying my patience."
"The witness will answer the question directly."
"Sustained." / "Overruled." / "The jury will disregard that last remark."
"I'm inclined to hold you in contempt, Mr. Sullivan. Choose your next words carefully."
"Bail is set at five thousand dollars."
"This court is not a circus, gentlemen. Act accordingly."
```

### 7.3 验尸官 / 法医（Coroner）

1920s 的法医科学还很初级，但 coroner 是正式官职。

```
"Cause of death: gunshot wound to the chest. Two entry wounds, no exit."
"Time of death, approximately between ten p.m. and midnight."
"The body shows signs consistent with a struggle."
"I've seen a lot of stiffs in my day, but this one... this one's different."
"No signs of forced entry. Whoever did this, the victim let them in."
"The bruising pattern suggests he was restrained before death."
"Officially, it's suicide. Unofficially... well, I don't get paid to have opinions."
```

## 八、职业间的对话动态

### 8.1 侦探 vs 律师（对抗性对话）

```
Detective: "Your client was seen leaving the building at eleven-fifteen."
Lawyer: "Seen by whom? A reliable witness, or another one of your 'informants'?"
Detective: "Reliable enough."
Lawyer: "In my experience, Detective, 'reliable enough' doesn't hold up in court."
Detective: "Neither does your client's alibi."
```

### 8.2 记者 vs 警察（信息博弈）

```
Reporter: "Any comment on the Hayward shooting, Sergeant?"
Cop: "No comment. Ongoing investigation."
Reporter: "I've got two witnesses who say your boys were on the take."
Cop: "Print that and you'll be writing obituaries for the rest of your career."
Reporter: "Is that a threat?"
Cop: "It's advice. Take it or leave it."
```

### 8.3 俱乐部经理 vs 侦探（回避与试探）

```
Detective: "I'm looking for a man named Vincent Morelli. Ring any bells?"
Manager: "We see hundreds of guests a week, Detective. Names... blur together."
Detective: "This particular guest had a bullet hole in him last Thursday."
Manager: "How distressing. I'm afraid I don't recall anything out of the ordinary."
Detective: "Your bartender says otherwise."
Manager: "Tony has a vivid imagination. Comes from reading too many dime novels."
```
